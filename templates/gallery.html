Wafer Defect Gallery

Explore different types of wafer defects and their characteristics

Edge Defects

Defects occurring at the edges of the wafer, often caused by handling or processing issues. These can affect the structural integrity and electrical properties of the wafer.

  • Common in wafer handling
  • Can affect wafer strength
  • May impact device yield

Donut Defects

Circular defects with a hollow center, typically caused by particle contamination or processing issues. These can affect device performance and reliability.

  • Ring-shaped appearance
  • Often caused by particles
  • Can affect multiple layers

Circle Defects

Perfectly circular defects that can indicate various issues in the manufacturing process, from contamination to etching problems.

  • Perfect circular shape
  • Multiple possible causes
  • Can be microscopic

Scratch Defects

Linear damage to the wafer surface caused by mechanical contact or improper handling. These can disrupt circuit patterns and affect device performance.

  • Linear patterns
  • Mechanical damage
  • Can be deep or superficial

Random Defects

Irregularly shaped defects that appear randomly across the wafer surface. These can be caused by various factors including contamination and process variations.

  • Irregular shapes
  • Random distribution
  • Multiple possible causes

No Defects

A perfect wafer with no visible defects. This is the ideal condition for semiconductor manufacturing.

  • Clean surface
  • No visible damage
  • Optimal condition

LOC (Line of Cuts)

Defects appearing as a line of cuts or scratches, often caused by mechanical stress or improper handling during the manufacturing process.

  • Linear pattern of cuts
  • Mechanical stress related
  • Can affect multiple dies
